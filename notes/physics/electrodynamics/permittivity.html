Permittivity

Permivity or absolute permitivity is the measure of the resistance that is encountered when forming an electric field in a medium. In other words, permittivity is a measure of how an electric field affects, and is affected by, a dielectric medium.

Permittivity is directly related to electric susceptibility, which is a measure of how easily a dielectric polarizes in response to an electric field. Thus permittivity relates to a material's ability to transmit or "permit" an electric field.

\begin{equation} \epsilon = \epsilon_{r} \epsilon_{0} = ( 1 + \chi ) \epsilon_{0} \end{equation}

where:

  • $\epsilon_{r}$ is the relative permittivity of the material
  • $\epsilon_{0}$ is the vacuum permittivity
