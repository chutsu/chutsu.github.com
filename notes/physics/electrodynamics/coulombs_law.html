Coulomb's Law

In 1785, Coulomb established the fundamental law of electric force $F$ between two stationary, charged particles. Experiements show that an electric force has the following properties:

  1. The force is inversely proportional to the square of the separation $r$, between two partciles and is directed along the line joining the particles.
  2. The force is proportional to the product of the charges $q_1$ and $q_2$ on the two particles.
  3. The force is attractive if the charges are of opposite sign and replusive if charges have the same sign.

\begin{equation} F = k \frac{q_{1} q_{2}}{r^{2}} \end{equation}

where $k$ is Coulomb's constant which is $8.9875 \times 10^{9} N \cdot m^2/C^2$, or:

\begin{equation} k = \frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \end{equation}

where $\epsilon_0$ is known as the permitivity of free space and has the value:

\begin{equation} \epsilon_{0} = 8.8542 \times 10^{-12} C^{2}/N \cdot m^{2} \end{equation}
